⚡ Hypersonics & Space Tracking
SciTec Tunes the Tracking Layer
Firefly Aerospace said its SciTec subsidiary won an AFRL contract supporting advanced algorithm R&D and verification architecture for global persistent awareness. The work emphasizes multispectral sensor-system research, deep-learning algorithms on size, weight, and power constrained processors, and improved target detection, tracking, and custody.
Why It Matters Missile warning is becoming as much a software and sensor-fusion competition as a satellite-count competition. If algorithms can maintain custody on dim or maneuvering targets at the edge, the U.S. gains decision time against hypersonic and other time-sensitive threats without waiting for every processing step to move through central ground infrastructure.

Polar OPIR Sensor Reaches Integration
Northrop Grumman delivered the prime sensor for the Space Force’s Next Gen OPIR Polar missile-warning satellite effort. The $4.15 billion two-satellite program is designed to improve northern-hemisphere warning and tracking by detecting faint heat signatures from ballistic and hypersonic threats.
Why It Matters Polar coverage is a persistent gap area for strategic warning because many high-latitude launch and flight profiles stress legacy architectures. Moving the prime sensor into the satellite integration flow turns Next Gen OPIR Polar from a paper architecture into deployable warning capacity for routes that adversaries would prefer to keep ambiguous.
The Defense Post: U.S. polar missile-defense satellite
Lockheed Joins the SBI Layer
Lockheed Martin was selected by Space Systems Command to develop space-based interceptor capabilities for the U.S. Space Force. The effort is aimed at an early-engagement missile-defense layer and draws on THAAD, PAC-3, Next Generation Interceptor, hypersonic strike, and missile-warning work, with an integrated demonstration planned by 2028.
Why It Matters Space-based interception remains technically and politically hard, but the program direction is clear: homeland missile defense is moving toward a layered architecture that couples sensing, fire control, and intercept earlier in the flight path. Lockheed’s entry widens the industrial base competing to prove whether this layer can transition from demonstration to credible operational architecture.

☢️ Strategic Nuclear Systems
NNSA Budget Climbs Again
A detailed FY2027 budget analysis says the administration’s defense request would give the National Nuclear Security Administration $32.8 billion, a 12% increase over FY2026 enacted appropriations. The same request includes broader nuclear-enterprise modernization, a Columbia-class ballistic-missile submarine, and continued B-21 development funding.
Why It Matters The nuclear enterprise is now competing inside a defense budget that is also demanding missile defense, AI, shipbuilding, and industrial-base expansion at scale. A double-digit NNSA increase signals that warhead sustainment and modernization remain protected priorities even as Congress scrutinizes the structure and affordability of the wider request.
Greenberg Traurig: FY2027 defense budget request
BWXT Locks In Reactor Work
BWXT secured more than $1.4 billion in Naval Nuclear Propulsion Program contracts. The package includes $1.29 billion for long-lead materials through the first of five annual task orders available through 2030 and $165 million for Ford-class carrier reactor-system components.
Why It Matters Strategic deterrence depends on the nuclear-propulsion industrial base as much as on missile and warhead accounts, particularly as Columbia-class and Virginia-class submarine production strains suppliers. Long-lead nuclear work gives the Navy and NNSA-adjacent industrial base schedule stability, reducing the risk that reactor-component bottlenecks cascade into submarine availability and recapitalization timelines.
Naval Technology: BWXT $1.4B naval nuclear propulsion contracts
Minot Focuses Sentinel Transition
Task Force 21’s North Dakota Nuclear Triad Symposium gathered senior military leaders, policymakers, and local stakeholders in Minot to discuss nuclear deterrence and modernization. Speakers emphasized Minot Air Force Base’s unique role hosting two legs of the triad and described Sentinel ICBM modernization as a generational national-security imperative.
Why It Matters Sentinel’s execution risk is not only technical; it also runs through communities, construction logistics, command-and-control transition, and workforce readiness at missile bases. Minot’s sustained focus matters because it is a front-line installation where the aging Minuteman III force, B-52 operations, and the future ICBM recapitalization effort intersect.

🤖 Artificial Intelligence
Compute Becomes the AI Bottleneck
DefenseScoop reported that the Pentagon is preparing additional steps to address compute as a major bottleneck for military AI proliferation. CDAO Cameron Stanley cited 13,000 targets in 38 days during Operation Epic Fury, a fourfold increase in network utilization, and 894 million tokens per day in agentic workflows.
Why It Matters The limiting factor for defense AI is shifting from model access to reliable classified compute, accreditation, and capacity across domains. If demand from operators outruns secure infrastructure, the Pentagon risks creating high-value AI pilots that cannot scale into routine battlefield decision support.
DefenseScoop: DOD AI compute bottleneck
Pentagon Sees Cyber Models as Defensive Leverage
Breaking Defense reported that senior Pentagon technology leaders are optimistic that Mythos-style frontier models can improve cyber defense. Cyber policy chief Katherine Sutton argued such models could help develop secure code, while CTO Emil Michael said they may find and patch vulnerabilities faster even as exploitation risk accelerates.
Why It Matters This frames frontier cyber capability as a race between automated exploitation and automated hardening, not simply as a safety-policy problem. Defense organizations that can safely integrate these tools into vulnerability discovery, remediation, and software assurance will compress patch timelines from weeks to minutes; those that cannot will be exposed at machine speed.
Breaking Defense: Pentagon officials on Mythos-style cyber AI
OpenAI Expands Trusted Cyber Access
OpenAI announced GPT-5.5-Cyber and expanded Trusted Access for Cyber for verified defenders. The framework lowers refusals for authorized workflows such as vulnerability triage, malware analysis, detection engineering, and patch validation while retaining blocks on credential theft, stealth, persistence, malware deployment, and unauthorized exploitation.
Why It Matters Frontier-model providers are building access-control regimes that look more like controlled cyber ranges than general chat products. For national-security users and critical-infrastructure defenders, verified access could unlock higher-value defensive automation while giving providers a governance mechanism to limit harmful dual-use spillover.
